## Example: Daily Journal Entry

**Filename:** `Crushed the Demo but Mom Called and I Spiraled.md`

```markdown
---
creationDate: 2026-03-15T21:30
type: journal
floor: Courage
floor_level: middle
gym: 3/4
sleep: 11:30pm
---

Today was a split screen. The product demo went better than I expected — the client actually interrupted me to say "this is exactly what we need." I felt it in my chest. Like okay, maybe I actually know what I'm doing. Diego stayed on mute the whole time which meant I carried it solo and it worked.

Then Mom called. She started with "I'm just worried about you" which is her way of saying she doesn't trust what I'm building. She asked if I'd considered "getting a real job again, just while things stabilize." I kept my voice steady but inside I was on fire. Not anger exactly — more like, why can't you just see me? I've been doing this for three years. I have a client. I have a team. But to her it's still a phase.

I didn't fight her on it. That's new. I just said "I hear you, Mom" and changed the subject. A year ago I would've gone into a 20-minute defense. Today I just... let it pass through me. That's Courage, not Pride. I didn't need to win.

The gym helped. Hit legs for the third time this week. There's something about squats when you're processing frustration — the weight gives it somewhere to go.

Tomorrow: follow up with the client, send Diego the recording, and don't call Mom back for at least two days.

*Floor: [[Courage]] · [[Middle Floors]]*
```

## Example: Weekly Insight Report

**Filename:** `2026-W11 Weekly Insight.md`

```markdown
---
creationDate: 2026-03-16
type: insight
period: weekly
date_range: 2026-03-10 to 2026-03-16
entries_analyzed: 5
primary_floor: Courage
floor_trend: up
gym_count: 3
avg_sleep: 11:15pm
---

## The Week at a Glance

5 entries this week (Mon, Tue, Wed, Fri, Sat). No gaps worth worrying about — Thursday was travel.

**Floor distribution:**
- Courage: 2 entries
- Love: 1 entry
- Fear: 1 entry
- Acceptance: 1 entry

**Primary floor:** Courage. **Trend:** Up from last week (Fear was primary). You moved from reacting to choosing this week. That's a real shift.

## What Stood Out

The client demo on Friday was a turning point. You carried it solo, it landed, and you let yourself feel it instead of immediately deflecting to "but what about the next thing." That's new.

The Mom call is a recurring pattern — she surfaces every 2-3 weeks and each time your floor drops. But this week you recovered in the same entry instead of carrying it into the next day. Last month the Mom calls would put you on Fear for 48 hours. This time: 2 hours.

You journaled about Diego twice — both positive. That relationship is stabilizing.

## Patterns a Life Coach Would Flag

- You hit the gym 3 out of 4 days this week. Last week was 1 out of 4. The weeks you move your body, your average floor is Courage. The weeks you don't, it's Fear. That's not a coincidence. Protect the gym.
- You mentioned "client pipeline" in 3 entries but haven't actually sent a single outreach email. You're thinking about it instead of doing it. What's one email you could send Monday morning?
- Your sleep is creeping later — 11:15pm average vs 10:45pm last week. Not dramatic yet, but worth watching.

## Patterns a Therapist Would Explore

- The way you describe the Mom call — "I didn't need to win" — that's a big shift from the pattern in your February entries where family conversations triggered a need to prove yourself. It might be worth sitting with what changed. Is it that you believe in yourself more, or that you've stopped expecting her to?
- There's a thread of loneliness running under the surface this week. You didn't name it directly, but three entries mention working alone, eating alone, deciding alone. The demo win was solo. The Mom call was solo. Even the gym is solo. Solitude and loneliness aren't the same thing — which one is this?
- You wrote "maybe I actually know what I'm doing" on Friday. The "maybe" is doing a lot of work in that sentence.

## Panel Thoughts

**Naval Ravikant:** "You're building leverage — a product that works without you in the room. The demo proved it. Stop measuring progress by how busy you feel and start measuring by how much works when you're not there."

**Brene Brown:** "The Mom conversation is your vulnerability practice ground. You stayed open without armoring up. That's not something you learn once — it's something you practice every time she calls."

**Peter Attia:** "Three gym sessions, sleep trending later, stress load increasing. You're in a phase where the body is your first early warning system. If sleep breaks 11:30 consistently, something else is slipping."

## Wins to Celebrate

- Carried the demo solo and it landed
- Didn't spiral after the Mom call
- Gym 3/4 (up from 1/4 last week)
- Recovered from Fear to Courage within the same day (first time this month)

## One Question to Sit With

You keep saying you want a co-founder. But every win this week was solo. Is the desire for a co-founder about the business — or about not wanting to be alone in this?
```
